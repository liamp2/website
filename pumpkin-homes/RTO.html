Rent-to-Own Program

Welcome to Pumpkin Homes – where our focus is to help families like yours purchase a home, (or keep an existing home) when you are having difficulty securing a conventional mortgage from the banks. Our RENT to OWN (RTO) program provides more flexible financing options than the banks and allows you to choose your own house. We purchase it, you rent it, and then we transfer ownership to you in 2-4 years at a price that is guaranteed regardless of how much the real estate market goes up.  With our system you get to enjoy your home earlier and know that up to 20% of your monthly rent goes towards your final down payment. 

Your family will benefit from a permanent location and will not have to deal with the upheaval (and expense) of moving regularly. If you have school-aged children you will appreciate being able to keep them in the same school, and with the same friends

Benefits of RTO

  • Qualify to buy a home NOW even with a poor credit rating
  • Repair your credit while having all the “benefits” of home ownership
  • Use our RTO program to refinance your home and clear your debts
  • Qualify for a better mortgage interest rate in the future when your credit score has improved
  • You only require a 3-4% deposit instead of 6-7 % if you were purchasing yourself (5% minimum deposit for CMHC insurance + legal costs + Land Transfer Tax)
  • Benefit from more reasonable qualification requirements for self-employed individuals
  • 20% of your rent is saved for the final down payment to purchase the home
  • Choose a house from almost any on the market in your pre-approved price range
  • Choose your neighbourhood and school zone for your children with no threat of relocation
  • Freedom to renovate, decorate, landscape and customize the home before you become the owner
  • Secure a guaranteed purchase price NOW and protect yourself against market increases
  • Benefit from equity appreciation from either your improvements of the home, or market increases

Six Steps to RTO Success

1. Qualification

We realize that you are having difficulty obtaining a mortgage on your own which be caused by a number of situations which do not necessarily reflect your true character or ability to pay. The following criteria will generally be required to qualify for our RTO program: 

  • Combined household income of at least $75,000 annually
  • A minimum down payment of $7,500 or 3% of purchase price
  • Stable, consistent income for at least 2 years for one applicant
  • Commitment to see credit repair specialist to improve your credit rating
  • Discharged from bankruptcy for more than 2 years
  •  Consumer proposal already in progress

2. Financial Approval

We will consult with you to identify the value of a home you can afford at the end of the 2-4 year period. Analyzing your financial information to ensure you would qualify at the end of term is crucial.  After verifying your employment, income and personal information we will approve you for our RTO program. There will be a non- refundable deposit of $500 at this stage to show your commitment, and to cover our costs of reserving the financing.

3. Choosing Your Home

This is the fun part but also the most important stage in the process. By this point you will know the price limit you are approved for and can take your time to find your perfect home. You can work with your existing real estate agent, or we can suggest a quality realtor from our network. There are a few guidelines we will review with you – such as no major “fixer upper”.

4. Purchasing the Home

Our team of experts - mortgage broker, real estate agent, lawyer, home inspector - will use their collective experience to make this a simple, quick and successful process. Pumpkin Homes will submit the offer and become the home owner and you become the tenant-buyer for 2-4 years. We will enter a Option to Purchase Agreement that details the final guaranteed purchase value. We will also sign an Occoupancy Agreement, which is very similar to standard rental agreement.

5. Enjoying the Home

 You enjoy all the benefits of home ownership during the rental term and can customize and improve your home. Any structural modifications to the home will require our approval, and if necessary the proper permits.

6. End of Term Purchase

Sixty (60) days prior to the end of the rental term we will be in touch to make arrangements for you to purchase your home as planned - at the guaranteed price agreed to in our agreement. The final steps as we transfer ownership to you are all pre-planned and very straight forward. Remember that your initial deposit, and your monthly down payment credits, together we will all contribute towards your final down payment. At this point your credit rating will be improved allowing you to qualify for your own mortgage.

RTO Frequently Asked Questions

​While we cannot anticipate all questions, below are answers to the most common questions you may have about RTO program. We are happy to provide a further explanation on any or all of the questions below. It is important to remember we are a small private company and deal with most situations on a case by case basis as there is never a 'one shoe fits all' solution in this business. Trust that we are not a rigid and bureaucratic bank. We are here to share our experience and help you realize your dream of home ownership.

How much of an initial deposit is required?
A minimum deposit of $8,000 or 3-4% of the initial purchase price will be required. This initial deposit will be credited 100% towards the  final down payment for your home.
How is my monthly rent set and how is it applied to the eventual down payment?
Your fixed monthly payment includes the following items: rent, property taxes, property insurance and a monthly down payment credit towards the final down payment. The monthly down payment credit is normally 20% of the total rent and is an amount always identified in the contract.
How much will I save for the final down payment?
The amount saved for the final down payment is the sum total of the initial deposit plus all the monthly down payment credits. The goal is to have 8-10% of the purchase price saved as a final down payment. As an example: if the initial purchase price was $270K then a 4% deposit would be about $11K, the final guaranteed purchase price after 3 years would be about $300K. The monthly savings towards your down payment credit would be $444 per month x 36 months=$16K. Thus your total savings towards the final down payment would be $27K ($11K + $16K) or about 9% of the final purchase price. We strongly encourage clients to accumulate a 10% down payment.
What is the typical rental period?
The typical rental period is 3-4 years. The term is based on how long it will take to repair or establish your credit, and the time it will take to save for your final down payment.
Can I make changes to the house and property during the rental period?
Yes. This is one of the many advantages of RTO program. You can paint, install laminate flooring, landscape, build a deck, install a jungle gym in the backyard and treat the house like your own. Any large or structural changes we will want to review and approve, but as long as it increases the overall value and appeal of the home, you will get the green light. If a building permit is required this will be your responsibility.
Could you help us stay in our existing home versus having to sell it to clear debts?
Yes – we call this our Home Refinance Option. The RTO program can be used to help you stay in your existing home, your existing neighbourhood, and keep your kids at the same school. The same process for qualification and approval will apply. We agree on a current purchase price and then confirm the value with a home appraisal. When closing the purchase we provide your lawyer with 93-95% of the agreed price to pay off your existing mortgage and debts. The remaining 5-7% is treated as an initial deposit for the final down payment. We have helped several families with our Home Refinance Option and keep their homes.
Why do you focus in Barrie, Simcoe County and northern York Region?
We prefer to meet all clients personally and remain involved during the rental period. We therefore prefer to help families in the communities near our home in Aurora. We are also better equipped to lend real estate experience in a market that is familiar to us.

We strongly believe the areas north of the GTA, from Newmarket and Bradford through to Barrie will continue to experience strong population growth, strong local economic growth, and also provide easy commuting distance to other employment areas in the GTA by car or the Go train. These demographic and economic factors have historically led to strong increases in home prices. These geographic areas still offer affordable housing prices today, and due to the reasons above should continue to experience positive increases in home prices in the future. Barrie specifically has been identified as one of the Top 10 Canadian cities for real estate investment for several years by REIN (Real Estate Investment Network).
What personal information is needed and who will this be shared with?
For the finance approval stage the same standard information requested for any loan will be required: two pieces of identification, 2 years of T4’s, 2 years of tax returns (NOA), full credit report, and an employment letter. This information will only be reviewed by a Pumpkin Home owner, our mortgage broker, or joint venture partner. It will only be shared with your approval.
Who pays for the home inspection and appraisal?
We initially pay for the home inspection and appraisal, and you would reimburse us within the first 60-90 days, since after all this will be your home. The home inspection is crucial since you will want to know as much as possible about its condition before we decide to make an offer. We will both review the results of the home inspection and make a mutual decision on how to proceed if there are any significant issues identified.
Who pays for property taxes?
Pumpkin Homes will pay the property taxes during the rental period.
Who pays for utilities and general upkeep?
You would pay for items just like any home owner. Examples of these normal costs are ALL utilities including hydro, water, gas, cable, phone, internet, and property maintenance like lawncare, leaf raking and snow removal.
Who pays for maintenance and large repairs?
You would pay for standard maintenance items just like any home owner. You would be responsible for standard maintenance items with costs up to $1,000 per occurrence (leaky toilet, broken dishwasher, broken window, etc) up to an annual value of $5,000. We will pay for any large expenses (broken air conditioner, roof) and then reach an agreement with you to be reimbursed over time, or at the end of term.
Who pays for house insurance?
This is actually a shared expense. Pumpkin Homes will pay for standard house insurance to cover the house, property and liability. During the rental period you will need to take out tenant insurance to cover your personal belongings in the house. Proof of tenant insurance will be required before you move in.
How do I improve my credit rating during the rental term?
We will discuss before entering into an agreement if your credit rating needs to improve before the end of the rental term. If improvement is required, we will need you to meet regularly (every 6 months) with a credit repair specialist of your choice to seek professional advice, and to provide us regular updates on the progress. This is important to ensure you have the best possible chance of qualifying for your own mortgage, at favourable rates, at the end of term.
What happens if I change my mind?
We have all committed a lot of time, energy and financial resources to helping you achieve your dream of buying a home so we really hope you do not change your mind. If you do, yes you can walk away at the end of the rental term, however you would lose your initial deposit as well as most of your monthly down payment credits, as defined in the contract.
